## Key Findings Summary

### Top 3 Validated Patterns

---

### Pattern #1: High Effort Required to Start Selling (Visibility + Marketing)

- Frequency: 9/10 interviews  
- Intensity: 4.5/5  

**Evidence:**
- "I'd have to post regularly… it just seemed like too much work." (Meg)  
- "Posting wasn’t enough — you have to understand the algorithm." (AN)  
- "I feel like I need everything to be perfect before I start." (DA)  

**Root Cause:**  
Selling depends on social media systems designed for engagement, not selling → creators must handle marketing, content, and growth themselves  

---

### Pattern #2: Lack of Structure in Selling Process (Orders, Communication, Organization)

- Frequency: 9/10 interviews  
- Intensity: 4.3/5  

**Evidence:**
- "Keeping track of who ordered what was confusing." (M.P)  
- "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." (KK)  
- "How do I categorize everything… my head just hurts." (Nita)  

**Root Cause:**  
No simple, centralized system exists for managing orders, products, and communication → everything is handled manually  

---

### Pattern #3: Trust, Pricing, and Emotional Barriers Prevent Sales or Starting

- Frequency: 10/10 interviews  
- Intensity: 4.6/5  

**Evidence:**
- "I didn’t know what price to put." (Studio138)  
- "Either underpricing or overpricing." (KK)  
- "If no one buys, it feels like my work isn’t good enough." (DA)  
- "Posting my work feels uncomfortable." (ND)  

**Root Cause:**  
Selling is emotionally tied to identity + no guidance on pricing or trust-building → leads to hesitation or complete drop-off  

---

## Problem Statement

### Final Evidence-Based Statement

Aspiring and small-scale handmade product creators in Georgia struggle to start or sustain selling online because the process requires high effort in marketing, lacks a structured system for managing orders and products, and creates uncertainty around pricing and customer trust. When attempting to sell through platforms like Instagram, they must handle content creation, communication, and organization manually, which becomes overwhelming—especially alongside other responsibilities. As a result, many delay starting, abandon selling early, or fail to convert interest into sales despite visible engagement. This leads to lost income opportunities, wasted time, and decreased motivation, with several creators stopping entirely due to stress and lack of results.

## Lessons Learned

### Discovery Process Learnings

1. Many users never start selling → onboarding problem is critical  
2. Emotional barriers are as strong as operational ones  
3. Workarounds (Instagram, DMs) are inefficient but widely used  

---

### What We Learned About Our Problem

1. Selling ≠ creating — completely different skill set  
2. Trust and pricing are major blockers to conversion  
3. Lack of structure leads to chaos → chaos leads to drop-off  

---

### What We Learned About Our ICP

1. Includes both:
   - Beginners (blocked from starting)
   - Active sellers (blocked from scaling)  

2. Pain intensity varies:
   - Beginners → fear & overwhelm  
   - Sellers → time & organization
