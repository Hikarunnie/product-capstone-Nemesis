## Decision-Making

**Day-to-day decisions** (formatting, tool choice, minor scope questions): any two team members can decide by discussion.

**Product direction decisions** (which problem we pursue, which user segment we target, what to build): discussed by all four, decided by simple majority. Tie situation: Program Lead makes final decision

**Major pivots** (abandoning our problem or significantly changing our solution direction): <br>
• Must have a proper meeting (at least 30 mins)<br>
• Decision is written and saved<br>
• No big decisions made in chat messages<br>

**Documentation:** Decisions affecting product direction are logged in `decisions/decision-log.md` (we will create this folder in Week 3 when we have our first such decision). Each entry records: date, decision, options that were considered, and reasoning.

## Conflict Resolution

We agree to follow the KIU 4-Step Conflict Protocol:

**Step 1 -- Verbal Discussion (15 minutes):** Each party states their position once and completely. Interrupting is not allowed. This means actually listening, not preparing a rebuttal.

**Step 2 -- Written View (10 minutes):** Each party writes a brief paragraph summarising their position and the core reason. This is shared with the other party (or with all three if the conflict is between two members). Giorgi has volunteered to facilitate this step if needed.

**Step 3 -- Cool-off (24 hours):** No decision is made immediately. The written views stay visible. In practice, we expect that re-reading the other person's written view after sleeping resolves most disagreements.

**Step 4 -- Program Lead Decision:** If still unresolved, member makes the final call as Program Lead. All parties commit to the decision. A dissenting view can be logged in the decision record but does not block execution.

**Escalation to instructor:** If the conflict involves member as a party, or if the 4-step protocol fails twice on the same issue, either party may contact zeshan.ahmad@kiu.edu.ge for mediation.

## Contribution and Accountability

**Tracking:** Every substantive contribution appears in the commit history. Documents and notes are committed by the person who created them. We do not batch-commit other people's work on their behalf.

**Lab attendance:** All three members attend every lab unless a genuine conflict exists and is communicated to the team at least 12 hours in advance. Missing a lab is the exception, not a habit.

**Deadline responsibility:** Ani confirms the repository is in a submittable state 3 hours before any deadline and posts a check-in message in WhatsApp. Ana confirms the submission tag is created and the email is sent. All other members have their individual components committed 6 hours before the deadline.

**Under-contribution:** After one missed deadline or two instances where a component was significantly below quality, the team meets to discuss explicitly. We will not raise it with the instructor without first having that direct conversation. Peer assessment at the end of the course will reflect actual contribution, not courtesy.

## Team Norms

- We write commit messages that say what is actually in the commit.
- We do not make decisions that affect the direction of the project via text message. Text is for coordination; decisions are for conversations.
- When we give feedback on each other's work, we are specific: "the WHO is too broad, it could mean two different segments" is useful feedback. "This is not great" is not.
- If one of us is struggling with something -- technically, personally, or otherwise -- we say so rather than going quiet. The team can only help if it knows.
- We treat the 11:59 PM deadline as the absolute last moment, not the target. We aim to submit by 20:00 on the deadline day.
