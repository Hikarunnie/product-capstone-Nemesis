## Team: Nemesis

### Ketevan Shavadze

#### H1 -- Hairband

**Problem:** <br>
Students struggle to wake up on time using basic phone alarms, which leads to missed classes, rushed mornings, and disrupted daily routines because alarms are easy to snooze, ignore, or turn off half-asleep.

**Where this observation comes from:** <br>
This happens to me a lot. I set multiple alarms, but I either snooze them without realizing or turn them off and fall back asleep. Sometimes I don’t even remember doing it. I’ve also seen friends miss early lectures for the same reason, even when they were actually motivated to go the night before. It feels like phone alarms just aren’t strong enough to match real sleep habits.

**Rough severity estimate: Medium** <br>
It affects daily routines and consistency, and over time it can impact academic performance and overall productivity.

---

#### H2 -- Headache

**Problem:**  
Students have difficulty staying focused while studying because of constant digital distractions (notifications, social media, messaging apps), which breaks concentration and makes simple tasks take much longer.

**Where this observation comes from:** <br>
I’ve experienced this while studying — even when I try to focus, notifications or the urge to check my phone interrupt me. What should take one hour turns into three. I’ve also noticed classmates constantly switching between studying and scrolling, which makes it harder to actually retain information or finish tasks efficiently.

**Rough severity estimate: High** <br>
This directly impacts productivity, learning efficiency, and time management, often leading to stress and unfinished work.

---

#### H3 -- Heartburn

**Problem:** <br>
Students living away from home struggle to maintain healthy eating habits because of limited time, budget constraints, and lack of easy access to balanced, affordable meals.

**Where this observation comes from:** <br>
I’ve seen this both in myself and others. During busy weeks, it’s common to skip meals, eat instant food, or rely on fast food because it’s quick and cheap. Cooking regularly feels like too much effort when you have assignments and deadlines. Friends often complain about low energy or feeling unhealthy but don’t have a convenient alternative.

**Rough severity estimate: High** <br>
This affects physical health, energy levels, and long-term well-being, which also indirectly impacts academic performance.

### Gvantsa Nozadze

#### H1 -- Hairband

**Problem:**
Students learning programming spend 30–60 minutes stuck on very small errors (such as syntax mistakes) because they cannot easily identify what is wrong, even when the problem is simple.

**Where this observation comes from:**
I experience this myself while studying coding. Recently, I spent around an hour trying to fix a single syntax error because I could not find where the mistake was. Situations like this happen often and slow down my progress significantly.

**Rough severity estimate:Medium**<br>
The problem does not stop learning completely, but it wastes a lot of time and creates frustration over small issues.

---

#### H2 -- Headache

**Problem:**
Students learning programming struggle to start solving tasks because they do not understand how to approach the problem, even if they know the basic concepts.

**Where this observation comes from: High**
I experience this during coding assignments where I understand the topic in theory, but when I see the task, I don’t know how to begin. This leads to spending a long time just thinking about where to start instead of actually solving the problem.

**Rough severity estimate: High**<br>
This slows down progress and reduces confidence, especially when tasks take longer than expected to begin.

---

#### H3 -- Heartburn

**Problem:**
Students trying to stay consistent with studying lose motivation when they do not understand material on the first try, leading them to stop studying instead of continuing.

**Where this observation comes from:**
I experience this personally. When I do not understand something quickly, I lose motivation and find it difficult to continue studying, especially when I already feel low on time and energy.

**Rough severity estimate: High**<br>
This affects long-term consistency and makes it harder to build strong study habits.

### Ani Kharabadze

#### H1 -- Hairband

**Problem:** <br>
Students miss good courses, internships, and opportunities because information is spread across LinkedIn, Instagram, university chats, and different websites, and they don’t have time to constantly check everything or simply, they don't know where to look.

**Where this observation comes from:** <br>
This happened to me when I found out about a really good course after the deadline had already passed. It was apparently posted on social media, but I wasn’t scrolling because I was busy with assignments and nobody shared either. On LinkedIn, my feed is full of random posts, so important things get lost easily. This has happened more than once, and sometimes I only hear about opportunities from others when it’s too late.

**Rough severity estimate: Medium** <br>
It doesn’t affect daily tasks, but missing even one good opportunity can have a big impact on future learning and career chances.

---

#### H2 -- Headache

**Problem:**  
Students who feel anxious about being judged by instructors or TAs avoid asking questions or showing their work, which makes them fall behind and spend way more time stuck on problems they could solve faster with help.

**Where this observation comes from:** <br>
I experience this myself and have also seen it in classmates. There were times I didn’t ask questions or didn’t want to show my homework to a TA because I was scared it would look bad, was wrong or affect my grade. Because of that, I spent a lot more time trying to figure things out alone and doing required presentations to TAs last minute, almost losing points. I’ve also seen classmates stay quiet even when they don’t understand something, being reluctant to speak.

**Rough severity estimate: High** <br>
This affects learning directly and creates stress, and over time it leads to bigger gaps in understanding and worse performance.

#### H3 -- Heartburn

**Problem:** <br>
Independent artists and small creative business owners in Georgia who sell products such as crochet items, jewelry, and paintings through Instagram struggle to organize, price, and sell their work consistently because there is no trusted, structured local platform that supports product display, pricing guidance, and reliable delivery.

**Where this observation comes from:** <br>
A close friend of mine recently started a crochet business on Instagram and wants to expand into other handmade items. I’ve seen them handle everything through DMs — answering questions, discussing prices, and managing orders. They often don’t know how to price their work and feel like people don’t fully trust Instagram sellers. It’s also hard for them to get noticed without constantly posting and not gaining followers makes their page look less trustworthy.

**Rough severity estimate: High** <br>
This makes it hard for small creators to grow, showcase their art, earn stable income, and manage their business efficiently.

**Where this observation comes from:**

**Where this observation comes from:**
I experience this personally when preparing for exams. Even after understanding topics during lectures or study sessions, I tend to forget details later and need to relearn them. This happens frequently across different subjects.
**Rough severity estimate: Medium** <br>

#### H3 -- Heartburn

**Where this observation comes from:**
