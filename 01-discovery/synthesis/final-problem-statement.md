# Final Problem Statement

**Team:** Nemesis  
**Date:** April 8, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Based on:** 10 interviews conducted between April 3 and April 8

---

## The Problem Statement

### Complete Statement

> Aspiring and early-stage handmade creators in Georgia struggle to start and sustain selling their products online when using platforms like Instagram because the process lacks structure, requires managing everything manually, and exposes them to emotional risk. This problem occurs when they attempt to price, present, and sell their work, often leading to confusion, fear of judgment, and operational chaos. As a result, many delay starting, mismanage orders, or stop selling entirely despite having interest or demand, limiting their ability to generate income and build a creative business.

---

## Component Breakdown

### WHO (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Primary user:**  
Aspiring and early-stage handmade or artistic creators trying to sell products online

**Demographics:**  
Students or young adults (18–30), hobbyists or side-hustle creators, based in Georgia, often using Instagram as primary platform

**Key characteristics that make them vulnerable to this problem:**

1. Low experience in selling
   - Evidence: "It feels like a completely different skill set outside of painting." (ND)

2. Strong emotional attachment to their work
   - Evidence: "If no one buys, it feels like my work isn’t good enough." (DA)

3. Reliance on informal tools (Instagram DMs)
   - Evidence: "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." (KK)

**How many people have this problem (estimated):**  
Thousands of small-scale creators in Georgia using Instagram informally (based on widespread usage and interview consistency)

---

### WHAT (The Problem)

**The core problem:**  
Creators cannot effectively start or manage selling their products due to lack of structure, unclear processes, and emotional barriers.

**How it manifests:**

1. Delaying or avoiding starting
   - Evidence: "I feel like I need everything to be perfect before I start." (DA)

2. Confusion and chaos in managing orders
   - Evidence: "Orders could get confusing." (AN)

3. Stopping after initial attempts
   - Evidence: "I stopped posting because I didn't know what price to put." (Studio138)

**Frequency:**  
Occurs every time users attempt to start selling or manage sales (high-frequency trigger event)

**Evidence of frequency:**  
"I posted my products, but after that I didn’t really know what to do next." (KK)

---

### WHEN / WHERE (Context)

**When does this problem occur?**

- When trying to start selling for the first time
- When posting products online
- When managing orders, pricing, or communication

**Where does it happen?**

- Primarily on Instagram and Facebook
- In direct messages and informal communication channels

**Evidence:**

1. "There are too many platforms, and I don’t know which one is actually worth using." (DA)
2. "I wanted to have a Facebook page but it needed lots of things so I just made a group." (Barbelle)

---

### WHY (Root Cause)

**Root cause:**  
Existing platforms are not designed for beginner sellers and require users to handle selling, pricing, trust-building, and operations manually without guidance or structure.

**Why existing solutions fail:**

1. Instagram DMs fail because they are unstructured and chaotic
   - "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." (KK)

2. Social media platforms fail because they require marketing and algorithm understanding
   - "Posting wasn’t enough — you have to understand the algorithm." (AN)

---

### IMPACT (Consequences)

**Quantified impact:**

- Time lost: Several hours per week managing messages, posting, and organizing manually (MP, Barbelle)
- Money lost: Missed sales despite interest
  - Evidence: "Posts got over 2000 views… but no one bought." (Studio138)
- Emotional cost: Fear, frustration, loss of confidence
  - Evidence: "The fact that my post wasn’t getting likes really affected me." (MB)
- Opportunity cost: Not starting or stopping selling entirely
  - Evidence: "I just stop." (Nita)

**Aggregate impact:**  
If thousands of creators repeatedly delay or stop selling due to these barriers, the total lost economic and creative potential is significant across the market.
