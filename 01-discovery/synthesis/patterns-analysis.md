## Pattern 1: Psychological Barrier to Starting (Rank #1)

**Frequency:** 7 of 10 interviews (DA, ND, MB, Meg, Studio138, Nita, KK)

**Pain Intensity:** 5 / 5

**Supporting quotes:**

1. "I feel like I need everything to be perfect before I start." (DA)  
2. "Posting my work and asking people to buy it feels uncomfortable." (ND)  
3. "If no one buys, it feels like my work isn’t good enough." (DA)  

**Emotional language observed:** "uncomfortable," "not good enough," "overthinking," "afraid"

**Consequences reported:**
- Delayed or no start at all  
- Fear of rejection prevents action  
- Strong emotional attachment to selling outcomes  

### Five Whys for Pattern 1

**Surface problem:** Creators do not start selling despite wanting to.

**Why #1:** Why do creators not start?  
Answer: They feel unready and afraid of failure. (DA, ND)

**Why #2:** Why do they feel unready?  
Answer: They believe everything must be perfect before launching. (DA)

**Why #3:** Why does perfection matter?  
Answer: Selling is tied to self-worth and validation. (DA, MB)

**Why #4:** Why is validation important?  
Answer: Lack of sales is interpreted as personal failure. (DA, MB)

**Why #5:** Why is failure so impactful?  
Answer: There is no safe, low-risk way to test selling. (ND)

**Root cause:** Selling is perceived as emotional exposure, not just a functional activity.

### Current Workarounds

| Workaround | Who Uses It | How Well It Works | Why It Falls Short |
|-----------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Delaying selling | DA, ND | Avoids stress | Prevents any progress |
| Posting without selling intent | MB | Reduces pressure | No revenue generated |
| Waiting for “perfect moment” | Multiple | Feels safer | Never leads to action |

## Pattern 2: Lack of Structure/System for Selling (Rank #2)

**Frequency:** 7 of 10 interviews (KK, AN, MP, Barbelle, Studio138, ND, DA)

**Pain Intensity:** 4 / 5

**Supporting quotes:**

1. "I didn’t really know what to do next." (KK)  
2. "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." (KK)  
3. "Orders could get confusing." (AN)  

**Emotional language observed:** "confusing," "stressful," "messy"

**Consequences reported:**
- Lost or mixed-up orders  
- Stress from manual processes  
- Reduced motivation to continue  

### Five Whys for Pattern 2

**Surface problem:** Sellers struggle to manage orders and workflow.

**Why #1:** Why is managing orders difficult?  
Answer: There is no structured system. (KK, AN)

**Why #2:** Why is there no system?  
Answer: Sellers rely on Instagram DMs. (KK, AN)

**Why #3:** Why rely on DMs?  
Answer: Instagram is the easiest starting point.

**Why #4:** Why not use better tools?  
Answer: Alternatives feel complex or unknown.

**Why #5:** Why are alternatives complex?  
Answer: They are not designed for beginners.

**Root cause:** Beginner sellers lack access to simple, structured selling tools.

### Current Workarounds

| Workaround | Who Uses It | How Well It Works | Why It Falls Short |
|-----------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Instagram DMs | Most participants | Easy to start | Becomes chaotic |
| Manual tracking (notes) | KK, MP | Temporary solution | Not scalable |
| Memory-based tracking | Beginners | No setup needed | Leads to errors |

## Pattern 3: Platform Overwhelm & Complexity (Rank #3)

**Frequency:** 6 of 10 interviews (DA, Nita, Meg, Studio138, Barbelle, ND)

**Pain Intensity:** 4 / 5

**Supporting quotes:**

1. "There are too many platforms." (DA)  
2. "My head just hurts thinking about organizing everything." (Nita)  
3. "It just seemed like too much work." (Meg)  

**Emotional language observed:** "overwhelming," "too much," "confusing"

**Consequences reported:**
- Decision paralysis  
- Not starting at all  
- Abandoning setup process  

### Five Whys for Pattern 3

**Surface problem:** Users feel overwhelmed by platforms.

**Why #1:** Why overwhelmed?  
Answer: Too many options and decisions. (DA)

**Why #2:** Why too many decisions?  
Answer: Platforms require setup, categorization, branding. (Nita)

**Why #3:** Why is that difficult?  
Answer: Users lack selling experience.

**Why #4:** Why no guidance?  
Answer: Platforms assume prior knowledge.

**Why #5:** Why designed like that?  
Answer: Platforms target experienced sellers.

**Root cause:** Selling platforms are not built for beginners.

### Current Workarounds

| Workaround | Who Uses It | How Well It Works | Why It Falls Short |
|-----------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Sticking to Instagram only | Most users | Simple entry | Limited functionality |
| Avoiding categorization | Nita, Studio138 | Reduces effort | Creates confusion |
| Not starting at all | Multiple | Avoids overwhelm | No outcome |

## Pattern 4: Pricing Uncertainty (Rank #4)

**Frequency:** 5 of 10 interviews (KK, MB, Studio138, MP, Barbelle)

**Pain Intensity:** 4 / 5

**Supporting quotes:**

1. "I don’t really know what’s correct." (KK)  
2. "You don’t want it to feel like slavery... but also not ripping off." (MB)  
3. "I didn’t know what price to put." (Studio138)  

**Emotional language observed:** "uncertain," "conflicted," "pressure"

**Consequences reported:**
- Delayed selling  
- Incorrect pricing decisions  
- Reduced confidence  

### Five Whys for Pattern 4

**Surface problem:** Sellers struggle to price products.

**Why #1:** Why is pricing hard?  
Answer: No clear reference or guidance. (KK)

**Why #2:** Why no reference?  
Answer: Handmade products vary widely.

**Why #3:** Why does that matter?  
Answer: Hard to compare with market.

**Why #4:** Why is comparison important?  
Answer: Fear of over/underpricing.

**Why #5:** Why fear pricing mistakes?  
Answer: Pricing affects perceived value and sales.

**Root cause:** Lack of pricing benchmarks and guidance for handmade goods.

### Current Workarounds

| Workaround | Who Uses It | How Well It Works | Why It Falls Short |
|-----------|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Guessing prices | KK, Studio138 | Quick decision | Often incorrect |
| Copying others | MP | Some reference | Not always applicable |
| Underpricing | Beginners | Safer feeling | Reduces profit |

---

## Cross-Pattern Analysis

Patterns 1 and 3 are directly connected: users do not start because the process feels both emotionally risky and cognitively overwhelming.

Patterns 2 and 5 are also linked: lack of structure leads to poor execution, which reduces visibility and trust, leading to fewer sales.

The strongest product signal comes from the intersection of Patterns 1 and 2: a solution that reduces emotional risk and provides a simple structured workflow would address the highest-impact problems simultaneously.
