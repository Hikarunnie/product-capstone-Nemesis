## Direct Competitors

Products or services that explicitly try to solve the same problem.

| Solution | Type | Mentioned By | What Users Said | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|----------|------|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|
| Instagram | App / Social platform | Interviews 1,2,4,5,6,7,8,9,10 | "I posted my products, but after that I didn’t really know what to do next." | Easy to start, high reach, familiar platform | No structure for selling, poor order management, hard to build trust |
| Facebook (groups/pages) | App / Social platform | Interviews 6,8 | "I wanted to have a Facebook page but it needed lots of things so I just made a group." | Simple entry point, community-based selling | Unstructured, messy communication, difficult to manage orders |
| Combined Instagram + Facebook usage | Informal system | Interviews 2,8 | "I had to figure everything myself… keeping track of orders was confusing." | Broad audience reach, flexible usage | No centralized system, high manual effort, inefficient workflows |

## Indirect Competitors

Products or services that solve a related problem or serve the same need differently.

| Solution | Type | Mentioned By | What Users Said | How It Relates |
|----------|------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| Personal branding (followers, aesthetic pages) | Strategy / social proof | Interviews 1,10 | "I check how many followers a page has before buying." | Helps build trust and credibility, increases likelihood of sales |
| Algorithm-driven posting (content strategy) | Social media mechanism | Interviews 4,7 | "You have to understand the algorithm." | Provides visibility and reach but requires effort and expertise |

## Manual Workarounds

Things people do by hand because no good solution exists.

| Workaround | Mentioned By | What They Do | Time/Effort Cost | Why They Tolerate It |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Managing orders through DMs | Interviews 4,5,8 | "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." | High — constant checking and tracking manually | No alternative system available |
| Manually building trust (following people, posting) | Interviews 1,7 | "I manually followed random profiles… it got tiring." | High — repetitive and time-consuming | Needed to appear trustworthy |
| Trial-and-error pricing | Interviews 1,2,8 | "I don’t know what price to put." | Medium — emotional + cognitive effort | No guidance available |
| Posting content consistently for visibility | Interviews 4,7,10 | "You have to post regularly." | High — ongoing effort | Required for algorithm visibility |
| Organizing products mentally or across pages | Interviews 1,9 | "Should I separate them into pages?" | Medium — cognitive overload | Platforms don’t support easy organization |

## "Do Nothing" Behavior

Users who have given up trying to solve the problem.

| Behavior | Mentioned By | Why They Gave Up |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Stopped posting/selling | Interview 1 | "I stopped posting because I didn’t know pricing and how to build trust." |
| Never started selling | Interviews 6,9,10 | "It feels like too much work." / "I keep delaying it." |
| Reduced effort or motivation | Interviews 4,7 | "It’s easy to lose motivation." |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Most common current solution:**  
   Instagram (often combined with Facebook) is the default platform for all users.

2. **Biggest gap in existing solutions:**  
   Lack of structure — no integrated system for orders, pricing, trust-building, and communication.

3. **Strongest workaround signal:**  
   Manual order tracking via DMs — high pain, high frequency, and directly tied to selling success.

4. **Your opportunity:**  
   Build an **all-in-one platform** that simplifies selling by combining:
   - structured order management  
   - pricing guidance  
   - built-in trust mechanisms  
   - low-effort onboarding and product organization
