## Clusters

Name each cluster as a **problem statement**, not a category label.  

---

### Cluster 1: Sellers struggle to manage orders, messages, and workflows in an organized way

Sticky count: ~12  

Representative quotes:
- "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." (Interview #5)
- "Keeping track of who ordered what… was confusing and time-consuming." (Interview #8)
- "Orders could get confusing." (Interview #4)

What this cluster suggests:  
There is a strong need for a **centralized system** that combines orders, communication, and tracking. Current tools (Instagram/Facebook DMs) are not designed for structured selling, leading to chaos and inefficiency.

---

### Cluster 2: Sellers feel overwhelmed by the effort required to sell (marketing, posting, communication)

Sticky count: ~10  

Representative quotes:
- "I'd have to post regularly… it just seemed like too much work." (Interview #10)
- "It's hard to advertise… constantly posting… I’m not good at those." (Interview #7)
- "Posting wasn’t enough — you have to understand the algorithm." (Interview #4)

What this cluster suggests:  
The effort required to maintain visibility and engagement is **too high**, especially for small or hobby sellers. This creates a barrier to entry and leads to abandonment.

---

### Cluster 3: Sellers lack confidence in pricing and communicating value

Sticky count: ~8  

Representative quotes:
- "I don’t know what price to put on my products." (Interview #1)
- "You don’t want it to feel like slavery… but also not like you’re ripping off the customer." (Interview #2)
- "People don’t understand the time and effort… which makes pricing more challenging." (Interview #8)

What this cluster suggests:  
Pricing is not just a technical issue — it is **emotional and tied to self-worth**. Sellers need guidance, validation, or benchmarks to feel confident pricing their work.

---

### Cluster 4: Sellers struggle to build trust and appear legitimate to customers

Sticky count: ~8  

Representative quotes:
- "The page didn’t seem safe… I also check followers before buying." (Interview #1)
- "There’s no platform that guarantees visibility or makes it easy to build customer trust." (Interview #10)
- "I wish there was something that guided me step by step." (Interview #6)

What this cluster suggests:  
Trust is a major blocker for conversion. Even when there is interest, **lack of credibility prevents purchases**. Platforms do not provide built-in trust mechanisms for new sellers.

---

### Cluster 5: Sellers experience psychological barriers (fear, perfectionism, low motivation)

Sticky count: ~7  

Representative quotes:
- "If no one buys, it feels like my work isn’t good enough." (Interview #6)
- "Posting my work… feels uncomfortable." (Interview #3)
- "I need everything to be perfect before I start." (Interview #6)

What this cluster suggests:  
Selling is emotionally risky. Fear of rejection, perfectionism, and discomfort with self-promotion significantly reduce action and consistency.

---

### Cluster 6: Multi-product creators are overwhelmed by organizing and presenting their products

Sticky count: ~5  

Representative quotes:
- "Should I separate them into pages… it feels like too much trouble." (Interview #9)
- "Instagram might confuse customers." (Interview #1)
- "Thinking about organizing everything makes me give up." (Interview #9)

What this cluster suggests:  
Platforms do not support **easy multi-category selling**, causing cognitive overload. This prevents creators from even starting.

---

### Cluster 7: Time constraints limit sellers’ ability to manage selling alongside other responsibilities

Sticky count: ~5  

Representative quotes:
- "I do it late at night after finishing my main job." (Interview #7)
- "I can do it whenever I have spare time." (Interview #10)

What this cluster suggests:  
Selling must fit into a **low-time, low-effort workflow**. Any system requiring constant attention or effort is unsustainable.

---

## Pattern Analysis

**Largest cluster and what it tells you:**  
Cluster 1 (workflow/organization chaos)  
→ The biggest problem is not discovery or creativity, but **managing the selling process itself**.

---

**Most emotionally charged cluster**  
Cluster 5 (psychological barriers)  
→ Strong language around fear, self-worth, and discomfort shows that **emotional friction is as important as functional friction**.

---

**Unexpected cluster**  
Cluster 6 (multi-product organization problem)  
→ Organizing products alone can prevent people from starting.

---

**Cluster that contradicts your original hypothesis:**  
Cluster 3 (pricing)  
→ Pricing is not just technical — it is emotional and identity-related.

---

## Preliminary Problem Statement (draft)

> Beginner and small-scale handmade sellers struggle to consistently sell their products online because existing platforms require high effort for marketing, trust-building, and workflow management, which results in confusion, low confidence, and eventual abandonment of selling.

---

## What We Need More Data On

1. What specific features would make users actually start (not just say they would)?
2. How much automation vs control do sellers want in managing orders and communication?
3. What trust signals most impact buyer decisions?
