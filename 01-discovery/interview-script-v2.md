## Opening (2 to 3 minutes)

"Thank you for speaking with me today. My name is [name] and I am working on a student research project at Kutaisi International University. We are trying to understand how creative people approach selling handmade or artistic work online and what gets in the way.

This is not a sales call and there is nothing to sign up for. I just want to learn from your experience.

There are no right or wrong answers. If I ask something that does not make sense, just say so.

With your permission I would like to take notes. Is that okay?

Let us start. Can you tell me a little about what you make, how often, and what you currently do with your work?"

---

## Core Questions (5 to 8 questions)

**Question 1:**

> "Walk me through exactly what you do — or what you would do — to sell one of your products online, from the moment it is finished to the moment someone pays you."

Why in v2: Produces the most concrete behavioral insight and reveals gaps in process.  
What you are listening for: Steps, missing steps, uncertainty, tools used (DMs, posts).  
Follow-up probe: "Can you tell me more about that?" / "What part of that feels most unclear to you?"

---

**Question 2:**

> "What has been the most frustrating or confusing part of selling your products — or trying to get started?"

Why in v2: Focuses purely on operational friction.  
What you are listening for: Order tracking issues, platform confusion, workflow gaps.  
Follow-up probe: "Walk me through a specific time that happened."

---

**Question 3:**

> "Is there anything about selling that feels personally uncomfortable — like posting your work, asking for money, or seeing how people react?"

Why in v2: Separates emotional barriers from operational ones.  
What you are listening for: Fear of judgment, rejection, low engagement sensitivity.

---

**Question 4:**

> "How have you decided what price to put on your products — or how would you decide?"

Why in v2: Pricing was a major blocker across interviews.  
What you are listening for: Guessing, copying, underpricing, lack of confidence.

---

**Question 5:**

> "Have you used Instagram, Facebook, or any other platform to try to sell or show your work? What was that experience like?"

Why in v2: Captures real behavior with current tools.  
What you are listening for: What happened after posting, visibility issues, drop-off points.

---

**Question 6 (optional):**

> "When someone looks at your page or your work online, what do you think they see? Has that ever affected whether someone buys?"

Why in v2: Surfaces trust and perception gap.  
What you are listening for: Trust issues, professionalism concerns, follower count impact.

---

**Question 7 (optional):**

> "Have you ever stopped or slowed down trying to sell? What was happening at that time?"

Why in v2: Identifies drop-off triggers.  
What you are listening for: Burnout, no results, overwhelm, lack of structure.

---

## Closing (1 to 2 minutes)

"We are coming up on time. Two last questions.

Is there anything about selling handmade or artistic products that you think I should have asked but did not?

Is there anyone else you know who deals with this that you think I should speak with?"

Thank the participant. Do not pitch your solution. If they ask what you are building:  
"We are still in the research phase -- we want to understand the problem fully before deciding what to build."

## Pre-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Read the full script 10 minutes before
- [ ] Blank notes document open
- [ ] Ask about the past, not the future
- [ ] Do not mention your solution or any feature ideas
- [ ] Silence is okay -- count to 5 before rephrasing a question
