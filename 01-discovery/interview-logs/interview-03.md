## The Story

Barbelle is a handmade candle seller who creates candles in different colors and forms depending on the season or upcoming holidays. This shows that she is actively involved in creative product making and changes her products based on customer demand or seasonal opportunities.

Selling candles is not her full-time job. She explained that she usually makes candles late at night after finishing her main job. This means time is one of her biggest constraints. She is motivated to continue selling, but the process has to fit around her work schedule.

Barbelle also talked about difficulty setting up a proper selling presence. She wanted to create a Facebook page, but it felt like it required too much work, so she created a Facebook group instead. This suggests that even basic platform setup can feel overwhelming for small handmade sellers who do not have much time or experience with online selling.

Her biggest frustration appears to be advertising and customer communication. She said that constantly posting and taking pictures is hard for her, especially because she does not feel skilled at those tasks. However, the most annoying part for her is texting customers. She said she does not know how to offer other products or motivate customers to buy her candles. This shows that the selling process is not only about showing products, but also about knowing how to communicate professionally and confidently with buyers.

Overall, Barbelle’s story shows that handmade sellers with full-time jobs may struggle more with time, advertising, and customer communication than with actually creating the product.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** Barbelle is already selling handmade candles and has experienced real problems with time, advertising, platform setup, and customer communication. The pain is strong because she is balancing selling with a main job and finds customer texting especially frustrating. However, the interview does not include evidence of repeated lost orders or a failed selling system, so the pain is rated 4/5 rather than 5/5.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Barbelle’s biggest barrier is not making candles, but managing the time-consuming selling tasks around advertising, posting, and communicating with customers.

---

## Current Workaround

- Makes candles late at night after her main job
- Uses a Facebook group instead of a Facebook page
- Posts and takes product pictures manually
- Texts customers herself, even though she feels unsure how to promote products or encourage purchases

**Why it’s inadequate:** The current process depends too much on manual effort, confidence, and time. A Facebook group is easier to start than a page, but it does not solve advertising, product presentation, or customer communication problems.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Customer communication may be more frustrating than product creation
- Some sellers avoid Facebook pages because setup feels too complicated
- Time pressure is a major issue for sellers who also have a main job
- Sellers may need help not only with visibility, but also with what to say to customers
