## Interview Setting

The interview was conducted remotely in an informal setting. The participant openly discussed their current selling process and challenges managing a growing handmade product business through online channels.

---

## Verbatim Quotes

**Quote 1**  
Context: Asked about creative process and products.

> "My creative process focuses on designing and creating handmade leather products such as belts, wallets and bags."

What this tells us:
The participant actively produces handmade products so they strongly match with our target ICP.

---

**Quote 2**  
Context: Asked about production frequency and goals.
> ""I create products regularly, for like 10–12 items a day."

What this tells us:
The participant operates at relatively high production volume and is serious about scaling the business.

---

**Quote 3**  
Context:  Asked about current selling/management process.

> “When I first started selling online, I had to figure out everything myself. Initially, just posting on Instagram and Facebook didn’t get responses. I had to improve product photos, write better captions, and respond quickly to messages. Keeping track of who ordered what and ensuring everything was delivered on time was confusing and time-consuming.”

What this tells us:
Operational complexity across multiple responsibilities is a great burden.

**Quote 4 (optional)**
Context:  Asked what they would change.

> ""I'd like a simpler system where orders, payments and customer communication are all in one place."

What this tells us:
There is strong need for centralized order management tools.

**Quote 5**  
Context: Asked about other important selling challenges.

> "Many people don't always understand the time and effort that goes into each handmade item, which makes pricing more challenging."

What this tells us:  
Customer education and  communicating value are part of the pricing challenge.


---

## Key Observations

- Observation 1: The participant wants to build a strong handmade leather brand and expand online presence, but current manual workflows slow down growth and reduce focus on product creation and marketing.
- Observation 2: They are willing to pay fees for a better platform if it reduces manual work.
- Observation 3: They showed noticeable frustration when discussing manual order handling and customer communication, revelaing this is an pain point for them.

---

## Preliminary Insights

- Insight 1: More established creative sellers may prioritize workflow efficiency over discovery and marketing.
- Insight 2: Sellers may accept platform fees if the platform saves enough time and management effort.
- Insight 3: Pricing challenges are partly caused by customers undervaluing handmade craftsmanship.
