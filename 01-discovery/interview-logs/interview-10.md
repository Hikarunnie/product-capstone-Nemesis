## The Story

KK is a handmade jewelry creator who makes bracelets and rings. She has already tried selling her products through Instagram, but the selling process felt unclear and stressful.

KK explained that posting products was only the first step, and after that she did not know what to do next. She said, "I posted my products, but after that I didn’t really know what to do next." This shows that Instagram may help sellers publish products, but it does not give them a clear workflow for what happens after posting.

Pricing was another major challenge. KK said, "Sometimes I feel like I’m either underpricing or overpricing — I don’t really know what’s correct." This suggests that handmade sellers may struggle to price their work because they are unsure how to balance material cost, time, effort, and customer expectations.

KK also had problems managing orders through messages. She said, "People would text me randomly and I would forget who ordered what." This shows that using Instagram DMs as an order system can quickly become confusing, especially when messages are unstructured and spread across different conversations.

Although KK enjoys making jewelry, the selling side feels much harder. She said, "It’s fun to create, but selling feels stressful." This shows a clear gap between the creative part of the work and the business side.

Overall, KK’s story shows that handmade sellers need more than a place to post products. They need a simple structure for pricing, order tracking, and knowing what steps to take after publishing.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 5 / 5

## One-Sentence Insight

KK’s biggest barrier is not creating handmade jewelry, but managing the selling process after posting — especially pricing, customer messages, and order tracking.

---

## Current Workaround

- Posts handmade jewelry products on Instagram
- Waits for customers to message her
- Handles customer communication manually through DMs
- Tries to remember or manually track who ordered what
- Makes pricing decisions without a clear system

**Why it’s inadequate:** Instagram DMs are not designed for structured order management, and manual tracking makes it easy to forget customer details or feel overwhelmed.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Posting products does not automatically give sellers a clear next step
- Pricing uncertainty creates stress even after the seller has already started
- Random customer messages can quickly become confusing without an order system
- Handmade creators may enjoy making products but still feel discouraged by the business side
