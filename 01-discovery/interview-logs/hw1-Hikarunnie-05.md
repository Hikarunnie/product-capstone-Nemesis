## Interview Setting

The interview was conducted via call after initial outreach through Instagram. The participant had recently attempted to launch a crochet Instagram page and was reflecting on early struggles they experienced while trying to begin selling online.

---

## Verbatim Quotes

**Quote 1**  
Context: Asked about what they create and want to sell.

> "I make crochet clothes, but I also wanted to post paintings, jewelry, and plushies because I do a lot of different crafts."

What this tells us:  
The participant is highly creative and has multiple potential product lines but is unsure how to present them cohesively online.

---

**Quote 2**  
Context: Asked why they did not post all product types.

> "I felt like posting different things on Instagram might confuse customers."

What this tells us:  
Branding and presentation concerns affect what sellers feel comfortable listing. INstagram not allowing grouping and categorizing forces businesses to stick to one product.

---

**Quote 3**  
Context: Asked about initial page performance.

> "It was very nice, took some pictures posted them and first posts got over 2000 views in less than a day. everything seemed so easy/"

What this tells us:  
Demand/interest  exists even for new sellers if products are appealing.

---

**Quote 4**  
Context: Asked why sales did not happen.

> "People reacted to the posts, but I think they didn't reach out because the page didn't seem safe which I understand, even when I buy something online I first look at how new page is and how many follwers they have."

What this tells us:  
Trust concerns may prevent sale despite visible interest.

---

**Quote 5**  
Context: Asked what happened after posting.

> "I stopped posting because I didn't know what price to put on my products and how to seem more trustworthy without manually following every random profile I came across, Which I did but it got very tiring and annoying very soon"

What this tells us:  
Pricing uncertainty and difficulty establishing trust created enough pain to stop the participant from continuing their selling efforts.
---

## Key Observations

- Observation 1: The participant appeared enthusiastic about creating products but noticeably less confident when discussing selling and pricing details.
- Observation 2: They interpreted lack of purchases as a trust issue rather than lack of product interest.
- Observation 3: The participant seems motivated to sell but is blocked by uncertainty.

---

## Preliminary Insights

- Insight 1: New businesses may abandon selling early if they cannot confidently determine pricing.
- Insight 2: Visible engagement/interest does not necessarily translate into purchases when page doesn't seem trustworthy enough.
- Insight 3: New sellers may need guidance not only on selling logistics but also on branding and presentation decisions.



## JTBD Frame

> "The participant is hiring Instagram to do the job of showcasing handmade crochet products and attracting potential buyers when trying to launch a creative business. The emotional job is feeling confident and legitimate as a new seller. The social job is appearing trustworthy and professional enough for customers to feel safe purchasing."

## Interviewer Reflection

Most important thing learned from this interview:  
I learned that pricing uncertainty alone can be severe enough to stop new sellers from continuing their business efforts, even when there is visible market interest.

What to do differently next time:  
I should ask deeper follow-up questions about how sellers currently attempt to determine prices and what information would help them feel confident enough to list products.
