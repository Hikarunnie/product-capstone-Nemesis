## The Story

Studio138 is a beginner crochet seller who recently tried to launch a handmade crochet business through Instagram. They mainly create crochet clothes, but they also make paintings, jewelry, and plushies. Because they create many different types of handmade products, they wanted to post all of them online, but felt unsure about how to present them in a clear and professional way.

One major concern was that posting different product types on the same Instagram page might confuse customers. Studio138 said, "I felt like posting different things on Instagram might confuse customers." This shows that Instagram’s lack of clear grouping or categorization can push small sellers to focus on only one product type, even when they have more creative work they could sell.

At first, the Instagram page seemed promising. Studio138 took product pictures, posted them, and some of the first posts received over 2,000 views in less than a day. This suggested that there was visible interest in the products. However, engagement did not turn into sales.

Studio138 interpreted this not as a lack of demand, but as a trust problem. They said that people reacted to the posts, but likely did not reach out because the page did not seem safe. They also connected this to their own buying behavior, explaining that when they buy something online, they check whether the page is new and how many followers it has.

The participant eventually stopped posting because of pricing uncertainty and difficulty building trust. They did not know what prices to put on their products or how to seem trustworthy without manually following random profiles. They tried doing that, but it quickly became tiring and annoying.

Overall, Studio138’s story shows that new handmade sellers can have product interest and motivation, but still stop selling if they cannot confidently price their products, appear trustworthy, and organize their brand clearly.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 5 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** Studio138 stopped posting completely because of pricing uncertainty, trust concerns, and the difficulty of building credibility as a new seller. The pain is strong because it directly blocked the business from continuing, even though the first posts received high engagement and visible interest.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Studio138’s biggest barrier is not creating products or attracting attention, but converting early interest into sales by appearing trustworthy, pricing confidently, and presenting multiple crafts clearly.

---

## Current Workaround

- Created an Instagram page for crochet products
- Posted product photos manually
- Considered adding other crafts such as paintings, jewelry, and plushies
- Tried to gain trust by manually following random profiles
- Stopped posting after pricing and trust-building became too tiring

**Why it’s inadequate:** Instagram can create visibility, but it does not automatically solve pricing, trust-building, or product organization. Manually following people to seem credible is tiring and does not create reliable customer trust.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- High views and engagement do not guarantee purchases if the page does not feel trustworthy
- New sellers may judge their own credibility based on the same signals they use when buying online, such as follower count and page age
- Pricing uncertainty alone can be enough to stop a seller from continuing
- Instagram’s limited organization features may discourage multi-craft sellers from showing all their products
