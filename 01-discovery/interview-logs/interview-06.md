## The Story

MB is an aspiring crochet artist who wants to sell handmade crochet products. She described crochet as something that requires significant time and emotional effort, but said it is often not valued properly by customers or by the wider market.

A major challenge for her is pricing. She explained that handmade creators have to balance two uncomfortable feelings: charging enough so the work does not feel exploitative, but not charging so much that customers feel they are being overcharged. She described this tension by saying, "You don’t want it to feel like slavery for you, but also not like you’re ripping off the customer." This showed that pricing is not only a financial issue, but also connected to self-worth and guilt.

She also talked about how crochet is perceived socially. According to her, it is often seen as a "grandma hobby," which makes it harder for people to take crochet artists seriously. This affects how customers judge the value of handmade products and makes creators feel less legitimate.

Another important part of the interview was the emotional effect of social media engagement. MB said that when her post did not get many likes, it really affected her. This suggests that platforms like Instagram or TikTok are not only tools for selling, but also sources of validation. Low engagement can make creators question whether their work is good enough.

MB also mentioned that AI-generated crochet patterns make customers lose trust. This was an unexpected point because it shows that the handmade market is being affected not only by pricing and social media algorithms, but also by trust issues caused by AI content.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** MB described the problem as emotionally important, especially around pricing, low engagement, and feeling undervalued. The issue affects her confidence and motivation to continue selling or posting. However, the interview did not include strong evidence of repeated lost sales or a failed workaround, so the pain is intense but not confirmed as maximum intensity.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

MB’s biggest struggle is not only reaching customers, but feeling that her handmade work is valued fairly by both the market and the audience.

---

## Current Workaround

- Posts or considers posting crochet work on Instagram/TikTok
- Watches engagement metrics such as likes
- Tries to judge pricing based on what feels fair to both herself and the customer
- Relies on personal judgment rather than a clear pricing system

**Why it’s inadequate:** Social media likes are unreliable as validation, and pricing based on feelings creates stress because she does not have clear evidence for what customers are willing to pay.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Pricing is emotionally loaded and connected to self-worth, not just cost calculation
- Low likes can strongly affect motivation and confidence
- Crochet being seen as a "grandma hobby" reduces perceived value
- AI-generated patterns may make customers less trusting of handmade crochet products
