## The Story

ND is a hobbyist painter who is considering selling her artwork, but she feels hesitant about the selling process. She enjoys painting itself, but sees selling as a separate and unfamiliar skill. She said, "It feels like a completely different skill set outside of painting," which shows that making the art and marketing the art feel like two very different activities to her.

The biggest barrier for ND is not the technical process of posting online, but the emotional discomfort of self-promotion. She said, "Posting my work and asking people to buy it feels uncomfortable." This suggests that social media can feel risky because it exposes both the product and the person behind it.

ND also described a low tolerance for stress in the selling process. She said that if selling felt too stressful, she might stop trying altogether. This is important because it shows that beginner artists may drop off very early if the first steps feel overwhelming or emotionally unsafe.

Another important point was that the artwork feels personal. ND said, "It’s not just selling — it’s something you created." This means rejection or low interest may feel more painful than it would with a normal product, because the work is connected to the artist’s identity and effort.

Overall, ND’s experience shows that for beginner artists, the main problem is not only how to sell, but how to start selling without feeling exposed, awkward, or rejected.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** ND described selling as stressful enough that she might stop trying entirely. The pain is strong because it creates hesitation and could prevent her from taking action. However, since she is still only considering selling and has not described repeated failed attempts or lost sales, the pain is not rated as 5/5.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

ND’s biggest barrier is not painting or even using social media, but the emotional discomfort of turning personal creative work into something publicly sold.

---

## Current Workaround

- Hesitates before posting or selling
- Treats selling as separate from painting
- Avoids direct self-promotion because it feels uncomfortable
- Relies on personal confidence rather than a clear selling process

**Why it’s inadequate:** Avoidance reduces her chance of getting feedback, customers, or a first sale, and it does not help her build confidence in selling.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Selling feels like a completely separate skill from creating art
- Self-promotion is a bigger barrier than the actual logistics of posting
- Stress could cause the artist to stop trying to sell altogether
- Emotional attachment to the artwork makes rejection feel more personal
