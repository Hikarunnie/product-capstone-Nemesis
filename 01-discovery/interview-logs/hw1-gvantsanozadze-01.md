## Interview Setting
 
The interview was conducted via Instagram video call. Nita appeared enthusiastic about crafting but became frustrated when discussing selling because she creates a variety of products (crochet items, painted objects, small decor) and feels overwhelmed trying to organize them online.  
 
---
 
## Verbatim Quotes
 
*Quote 1*  
Context: Asked about what they create and why.
 
"I love making all kinds of things—crochet dolls, little paintings, bookmarks, tiny decorations. But sometimes I start thinking about how to show them online, how to categorize them, and my head just hurts."  

 
What this tells us:  
The participant enjoys crafting across multiple categories but struggles with mental load and organization when trying to present all their products coherently.  
 
---
 
*Quote 2*  
Context: Asked about challenges in selling online.
 
"I’d start making posts, then I’d wonder if people would understand that I sell many different things. Should I separate them into pages, categories, collections? instagram doesn't even support that kinda things so It feels like too much trouble and I just stop."  

 
What this tells us:  
The main barrier is cognitive overload from managing multiple product types; the effort of organization prevents them from ever starting to sell.  
 
---
 
*Quote 3*  
Context: Asked about their ideal selling scenario.
 
"If there were a way to just show all my crafts without having to think too much about categorizing, tagging, or posting  I’d actually start selling. Right now, thinking about organizing everything makes me give up before I even begin."  

 
What this tells us:  
They want a platform that reduces organizational friction and allows multiple product types to coexist without adding stress.  
 
---
 
## Key Observations
 
- Observation 1: Nita is a multi-craft creator who feels mentally blocked by the logistics of organizing products.  
- Observation 2: Cognitive overload is the main pain point, more than marketing or social media engagement.  
- Observation 3: Reducing operational complexity and categorization would likely unlock their ability to start selling.  
 
---
 
## Preliminary Insights
 
- Insight 1: Multi-category creators are discouraged from selling due to organizational complexity.  
- Insight 2: Tools that automatically group, categorize, or display diverse products could help small sellers begin selling.  
- Insight 3: Mental friction from planning product presentation can prevent motivated creators from starting to sell.
