## The Story

AN is a casual handmade seller who has already tried selling through Instagram. Unlike some beginner creators who are only considering selling, AN has direct experience with the process and has already encountered practical problems.

One of the main issues AN described was visibility. She said, "Posting wasn’t enough — you have to understand the algorithm." This shows that simply uploading products does not guarantee that people will see them. For handmade sellers, Instagram may look easy at first, but success depends on understanding platform behavior, timing, engagement, and consistency.

AN also talked about the difficulty of managing orders. She said, "Orders could get confusing," which suggests that Instagram DMs are not a reliable system for tracking customer requests, payments, product details, and delivery information. As more people become interested, the process can quickly become disorganized.

Another important point was motivation. AN said, "It’s easy to lose motivation." This connects the practical problems to an emotional consequence. When visibility is low, orders are confusing, or results are inconsistent, the seller may feel discouraged and stop trying.

The strongest summary of her experience was: "Selling is harder than creating." This shows that the main challenge is not making the handmade product itself, but handling the business side: posting, reaching customers, managing orders, and staying motivated.

Overall, AN’s story shows that Instagram can be a useful starting point, but it becomes difficult when the seller needs structure, consistency, and a simple way to manage the full selling process.

## One-Sentence Insight

AN’s experience shows that handmade sellers do not only need a place to post products; they need a simple structure for visibility, order management, and motivation.

---

## Current Workaround

- Uses Instagram posts to show products
- Uses Instagram DMs to communicate with customers
- Tries to understand the algorithm to improve visibility
- Manages orders manually without a clear system

**Why it’s inadequate:** Instagram is useful for reaching people, but it is not designed as a complete selling system. Manual DMs can become confusing, and low visibility or inconsistent results can quickly reduce motivation.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Posting products is not enough if the seller does not understand the algorithm
- Order management becomes confusing even for casual sellers
- Motivation depends strongly on whether the seller sees results
- The selling process can feel harder than the creative process itself
