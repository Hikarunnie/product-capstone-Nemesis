## The Story

Meg is a handmade bookmarks and keychains creator who enjoys making small craft items in her spare time. She likes these products because they are quick to make, manageable, and fit into her schedule. She said, "I've always loved making bookmarks and little keychains. I enjoy it because it's small, fast to make, and I can do it whenever I have spare time."

Although Meg would like to sell her products, she has not started because the selling process feels too demanding. She considered creating an Instagram page, but quickly realized that it would require regular posting, gaining followers, taking product photos, and writing stories. For small, low-cost handmade items, this effort feels disproportionate. She said, "I thought about creating an Instagram page, but then I realized I'd have to post regularly, gain followers, take pictures, write stories… it just seemed like too much work for something so small."

A major issue for Meg is that she does not have enough time to create bigger or more profitable art pieces, but selling small products still requires visibility and customer trust. She wants a way to sell without constantly maintaining a social media presence. She explained that her main problem is the lack of a platform that guarantees visibility or helps build customer trust without a lot of effort.

Overall, Meg’s story shows that some handmade creators are motivated to sell, but the marketing effort required by platforms like Instagram can make selling feel not worth it, especially for small and inexpensive products.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 4 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** Meg clearly wants to sell her handmade bookmarks and keychains, but the expected effort of social media marketing stops her from starting. The pain is strong because it blocks action completely. However, since she has not described repeated failed attempts, lost orders, or an existing selling system breaking down, the pain is rated 4/5 rather than 5/5.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

Meg’s biggest barrier is not making products, but feeling that the time and effort required to gain visibility and customer trust is too high for small handmade items.

---

## Current Workaround

- Makes bookmarks and keychains in her spare time
- Considers creating an Instagram page
- Avoids starting because regular posting, photography, stories, and follower-building feel too demanding
- Has no simple platform for low-effort visibility and trust-building

**Why it’s inadequate:** Instagram requires ongoing content creation and audience-building, which feels too time-consuming for a creator selling small, low-cost products.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- Small handmade products can feel “not worth selling” if marketing effort is too high
- The main barrier is visibility and trust-building, not product creation
- A creator may be motivated to sell but still not start because social media maintenance feels overwhelming
- Built-in visibility or credibility features could make selling feel more achievable for low-bandwidth creators
