## The Story

Nita is a multi-craft hobbyist who enjoys making different kinds of handmade products, including crochet dolls, little paintings, bookmarks, and small decorations. She is interested in selling her work, but she feels overwhelmed by the process of organizing different product types online.

Her main challenge is not lack of motivation to create. Instead, the problem appears when she thinks about how to present everything clearly to potential buyers. She said, "I love making all kinds of things—crochet dolls, little paintings, bookmarks, tiny decorations. But sometimes I start thinking about how to show them online, how to categorize them, and my head just hurts." This shows that the mental effort of organizing products creates a barrier before she even begins selling.

Nita also explained that Instagram does not feel flexible enough for someone who sells many different types of handmade items. She worries that customers may not understand what she offers if everything is posted together. At the same time, creating separate pages, categories, or collections feels like too much work. She said, "Should I separate them into pages, categories, collections? Instagram doesn’t even support that kinda things so it feels like too much trouble and I just stop."

Her ideal selling situation would be one where she could show all of her crafts without spending too much time thinking about categories, tags, or posting structure. She said that if organizing everything felt easier, she would actually start selling. This suggests that reducing organizational friction could help creators like Nita move from intention to action.

Overall, Nita’s story shows that multi-craft creators may be blocked not by creativity or product quality, but by the mental load of presenting diverse products in a coherent and professional way.

## Current Workaround

- Thinks about using Instagram to show her crafts
- Considers separating products into pages, categories, or collections
- Tries to mentally organize different product types before posting
- Stops before starting because the setup feels too overwhelming

**Why it’s inadequate:** Instagram does not provide an easy structure for multi-category handmade sellers, and manually deciding categories, tags, and collections creates too much mental friction.

## Unexpected Findings

- Cognitive overload can stop creators before they even attempt to sell
