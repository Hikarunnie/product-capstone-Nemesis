## Interview Setting
 
The interview was conducted via Instagram video call. The participant was comfortable speaking openly about their creative work. They emphasized that making bookmarks and keychains is a hobby, and that selling online feels intimidating because of the effort involved in creating visibility and trust.
 
---
 
## Verbatim Quotes
 
*Quote 1*  
Context: Asked about what they create and why.
 
"I've always loved making bookmarks and little keychains. I enjoy it because it's small, fast to make, and I can do it whenever I have spare time."

 
What this tells us:  
The participant enjoys crafting but focuses on small, manageable items due to limited available time.
 
---
 
*Quote 2*  
Context: Asked about challenges in selling online.
 
"I thought about creating an Instagram page, but then I realized I'd have to post regularly, gain followers, take pictures, write stories… it just seemed like too much work for something so small."

 
What this tells us:  
The participant is deterred by social media marketing overhead. For small, low-cost products, the effort of visibility and trust-building feels disproportionate.
 
---
 
*Quote 3*  
Context: Asked about selling motivation.
 
"I’d love to sell these, but I don’t have time to work on bigger or more profitable art pieces. My main problem is there’s no platform that guarantees visibility or makes it easy to build customer trust without a lot of effort."

 
What this tells us:  
The participant wants to sell but is constrained by time and lack of low-effort tools for exposure and credibility.
 
---
 
## Key Observations
 
- Observation 1: Meg has a strong desire to sell but has not started due to perceived operational troubles.
- Observation 2: The main barrier is not product creation, but visibility, marketing, and trust-building.
- Observation 3: A low-effort selling platform with built-in trust mechanisms would likely motivate them to start selling.
 
---
 
## Preliminary Insights
 
- Insight 1: Aspiring small sellers need platforms that minimize marketing effort while still providing visibility.
- Insight 2: Operational friction can prevent creators from ever starting to sell, even if they are motivated.
- Insight 3: The barrier to entry for selling small, low-cost handmade items is primarily logistical and social, not creative.

## Interviewer Reflection
 
Most important thing learned from this interview:  
Even motivated creators may not start selling if the perceived effort to maintain visibility and trust outweighs the perceived value of the product.
 
What to do differently next time:  
Ask more about what minimal effort would be enough to start selling and what specific trust-building elements would encourage them to take the first step.
