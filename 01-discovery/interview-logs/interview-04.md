## The Story

M.P. is a handmade leather goods crafter and seller who creates products such as belts, wallets, and bags. Unlike some beginner creators, M.P. is already producing regularly and operates at a relatively serious level, making around 10–12 items per day.

When M.P. first started selling online, they had to learn the full selling process independently. Simply posting products on Instagram and Facebook was not enough to get responses. Over time, they had to improve product photos, write better captions, and respond quickly to customer messages. This shows that selling handmade products requires many extra skills beyond product creation.

The biggest challenge for M.P. appears to be managing the selling workflow manually. They said that keeping track of who ordered what and making sure everything was delivered on time became confusing and time-consuming. This suggests that as the business grows, Instagram and Facebook become less useful as management tools because they do not centralize orders, payments, and customer communication.

M.P. also said they would like a simpler system where orders, payments, and customer communication are all in one place. This shows a clear need for a structured platform that reduces manual work and helps sellers feel more organized.

Another important issue was pricing. M.P. explained that many customers do not fully understand the time and effort that goes into handmade leather goods, which makes pricing harder. This means the pricing problem is not only about calculating costs, but also about communicating the value of handmade craftsmanship.

Overall, M.P.’s story shows that more experienced handmade sellers may need workflow efficiency and centralized management more than basic motivation or beginner guidance.

## Pain Intensity

**Rating:** 5 / 5

**Evidence for rating:** M.P. is already selling at a relatively high production volume and has experienced repeated operational problems with orders, payments, communication, and delivery tracking. The pain is strong because manual workflows directly slow down business growth and take time away from creation and marketing.

---

## One-Sentence Insight

M.P.’s biggest pain is not creating leather products, but managing a growing handmade business through scattered social media tools that do not centralize orders, payments, and communication.

---

## Current Workaround

- Uses Instagram and Facebook to market and sell products
- Improves photos and captions manually to increase engagement
- Responds quickly to customer messages
- Tracks orders and deliveries manually
- Handles pricing explanations directly with customers

**Why it’s inadequate:** Instagram and Facebook help with visibility, but they do not provide a complete system for managing orders, payments, customer communication, and delivery tracking in one place.

---

## Unexpected Findings

- More experienced sellers may care more about workflow efficiency than basic visibility
- Sellers may be willing to pay platform fees if the system saves enough time
- Customer undervaluing of handmade work makes pricing harder
- A centralized system could help sellers look more professional and feel more in control
