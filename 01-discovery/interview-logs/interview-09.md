## The Story

DA is a mixed media artist who creates both digital and handmade work. They are interested in selling their art, but they have not started yet. The main reason is not lack of skill or lack of interest, but hesitation and overthinking.

DA explained that they feel they need everything to be perfect before launching. They said, "I feel like I need everything to be perfect before I start, so I keep delaying it." This shows that perfectionism is stopping them from taking the first step. Instead of testing the market or posting imperfectly, they continue waiting until they feel fully ready.

Another barrier is platform confusion. DA said, "There are too many platforms, and I don’t know which one is actually worth using." This suggests that having many options does not always help new sellers. Instead, too many platform choices can create decision paralysis and make starting feel harder.

The emotional side of selling is also important for DA. They said, "If no one buys, it feels like my work isn’t good enough." This shows that selling is not just a business activity for them. It feels personally risky because low sales could be interpreted as rejection of their creative ability.

DA also said they wished there was something that guided them step by step. This suggests that a structured, beginner-friendly system could reduce anxiety and help them move from thinking about selling to actually starting.

Overall, DA’s story shows that some creative sellers are blocked before they even reach the selling stage. Their biggest problem is not managing orders or getting repeat customers, but gaining enough confidence and guidance to launch.

## One-Sentence Insight

DA’s biggest barrier is not creating art, but feeling confident enough to start selling without needing everything to be perfect first.

## Current Workaround

- Delays launching until everything feels ready
- Thinks about different platform options but does not choose one
- Avoids selling because lack of purchases would feel personally discouraging
- Looks for guidance but currently does not have a clear step-by-step process

**Why it’s inadequate:** Waiting for perfection prevents DA from testing demand, learning from real customers, or building confidence through small actions.

## Unexpected Findings

- The problem can begin before the user even starts selling
- Too many platform options can create decision paralysis
- Fear of no sales is tied to self-worth and artistic confidence
- Guided onboarding or step-by-step templates could help hesitant creators take action
